AI ASSISTED SWARM CUBESATS FOR ACTIVE DEBRIS REMOVAL IN LEO

OBJECTIVE

This study aims to formulate an economically viable and adaptively scalable framework for active debris removal in Low Earth Orbit (LEO). Central to this framework is the deployment of a swarm of CubeSats, each embedded with artificial intelligence for autonomous debris identification, rendezvous, and capture. A data-driven “LEO model” guides mission planning, dynamically adjusting CubeSat constellations to accommodate variations in debris geometry and orbital parameters. Ultimately, the research seeks to optimize on-orbit logistics and improve long-term orbital sustainability through robust swarm-based interventions.

CHALLENGES

The exponential growth of satellite missions has escalated orbital congestion, amplifying collision risks and threatening operational spacecraft. Achieving swarm autonomy under complex orbital dynamics demands advanced AI algorithms, real-time sensor fusion, and stringent fault-tolerance mechanisms. Balancing the CubeSats’ limited resources with the mission’s high-level tasks involves intricate energy management, precise navigation, and reliable inter-satellite communication. Moreover, establishing economic feasibility requires rigorous cost modeling and policy compliance, particularly as missions scale to address heterogeneous debris populations.

METHODOLOGY

Our methodology followed these steps:

A multi-agent architecture is employed, wherein each CubeSat leverages AI-driven situational awareness to form a cohesive constellation around targeted debris. High-fidelity simulations guide path planning, propulsion strategies, and dynamic capture techniques, anchored by real-time data assimilation from onboard sensors. Machine learning models, including geometric and dimensional analyses, inform debris engagement protocols and adapt the maneuvering profile for each capture scenario. Economic assessments, coupled with parametric trade studies, ensure that design decisions align with cost-effective mission implementation while preserving performance and reliability.
